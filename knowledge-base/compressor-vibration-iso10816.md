# Compressor Vibration Guide (sensor_002, ISO 10816-3)

## Vibration severity zones (RMS velocity, mm/s)
For medium machines (15–75 kW) on rigid foundations:
- **Zone A (good)**: 0 – 2.3 mm/s – newly commissioned condition.
- **Zone B (acceptable)**: 2.3 – 4.5 mm/s – unrestricted long-term operation.
- **Zone C (tolerable)**: 4.5 – 7.1 mm/s – not suitable for long-term operation;
  plan maintenance.
- **Zone D (severe)**: > 7.1 mm/s – vibration can cause damage; act immediately.

## Diagnosis by signature
- **1x running speed** dominant: unbalance.
- **2x running speed**: misalignment or looseness.
- **High-frequency broadband**: bearing wear or lubrication failure.
- **Sudden step increase**: mechanical looseness or a cracked mount.

## Recommended actions
- Entering Zone C: schedule balancing/alignment within the next maintenance
  window; increase monitoring frequency.
- Entering Zone D: raise a critical alarm, reduce load if possible, and
  inspect bearings and couplings before continued operation.
